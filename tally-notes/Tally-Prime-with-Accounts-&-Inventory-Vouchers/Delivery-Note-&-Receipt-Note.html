1. Introduction to Inventory Tracking

Receipt Note & Delivery Note record physical movement of goods without any accounting effect.

Updates: Stock Quantity | Pending Orders | Inward/Outward Status

Does NOT update: Ledger Accounts | Purchase/Sales Value | Trial Balance

2. Receipt Note (GRN – Goods Receipt Note)

Recorded when goods are physically received from supplier.

Linked to Purchase Order → Updates stock → Tracks partial receipts

3. Delivery Note (DN)

Recorded when goods are dispatched to customer.

Linked to Sales Order → Reduces stock → Tracks partial deliveries

5. Receipt Note – Theory & Practical

Effect: Stock ↑ | PO Pending ↓ | No accounting entry

Shortcut: Alt + F9

  1. Vouchers → Alt + F9
  2. Select Supplier
  3. Select Purchase Order No.
  4. Items auto-load → Enter Received Quantity
  5. Save

6. Delivery Note – Theory & Practical

Effect: Stock ↓ | SO Pending ↓ | No accounting entry

Shortcut: Alt + F8

  1. Vouchers → Alt + F8
  2. Select Customer
  3. Select Sales Order No.
  4. Items auto-load → Enter Delivered Quantity
  5. Save

9. Order Tracking Flow (Summary)

PURCHASE CYCLE
Purchase Order → Receipt Note → Purchase Invoice

SALES CYCLE
Sales Order → Delivery Note → Sales Invoice

10. Common Mistakes

  • Not selecting correct PO/SO
  • Receiving/Delivering more than ordered
  • Forgetting to enable Tracking Numbers
  • Wrong supplier/customer selection
  • Entering wrong quantity/unit

11. Revision Notes

  • Receipt Note = Stock In (No accounting)
  • Delivery Note = Stock Out (No accounting)
  • Must link to PO/SO for tracking
  • Updates pending order reports
  • Essential before creating Purchase/Sales Invoice
