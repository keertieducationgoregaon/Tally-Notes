1. Concept of Order Processing

Order processing records business commitments before actual purchase or sale occurs.

PO/SO entries do NOT affect ledger balances or stock — they only record intent.

2. Purchase Order (PO)

A formal request sent by the business to its supplier for goods.

Benefits: Clear documentation, prevents over-billing, tracks pending orders.

3. Sales Order (SO)

A confirmation document sent by the business to its customer.

Benefits: Confirms demand, plans stock, prevents dispatch errors.

7. Alteration & Cancellation

Alter: Day Book → Select PO/SO → Enter → Modify → Save

Cancel: Open PO/SO → Alt + X → Confirm

8. Order Tracking Flow

PURCHASE FLOW
Purchase Order → Receipt Note → Purchase Voucher

SALES FLOW
Sales Order → Delivery Note → Sales Voucher

9. Common Mistakes

  • Wrong supplier/customer ledger
  • Incorrect rates or quantities
  • Wrong stock item selected
  • No due date entered
  • Order processing not activated

10. Revision Notes

  • PO → To Supplier | SO → To Customer
  • PO/SO do NOT affect stock or accounts
  • Must enable in F11 → Inventory Features
  • Used for planning & tracking
  • Essential for Receipt/Delivery Notes later
