1. Introduction to Ledgers

A ledger in Tally Prime is an individual accounting account that records debit and credit transactions. Every business transaction requires at least two ledgers — one debit and one credit.

Examples of ledgers:

  • Cash
  • Bank
  • Sales
  • Purchase
  • Customer accounts
  • Supplier accounts
  • Expenses
  • Income accounts

In accounting terminology, the ledger is the final destination of all transactions. In Tally Prime, ledgers are organized under predefined groups, which determine how they behave in financial statements.

2. Purpose of Ledgers in Accounting

  • Accurate Classification of Transactions
  • Preparation of Financial Reports: Trial Balance, P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash & Bank Summary
  • Business Analysis: Expense tracking, Debtor/Creditor management, Bank reconciliation
  • Automation in Tally: Auto-update inventory, outstanding reports, cost of goods sold

3. Ledger Groups in Tally Prime

Every ledger must belong to a group. Tally has 28 predefined groups.

CategoryImportant Groups
AssetsCash-in-Hand, Bank Accounts, Current Assets, Fixed Assets
LiabilitiesCapital Account, Current Liabilities, Duties & Taxes
IncomeDirect Income, Indirect Income, Sales Accounts
ExpensesDirect Expenses, Indirect Expenses, Purchase Accounts

Correct grouping ensures accurate financial statements.

4. Rules of Ledger Creation

  1. Every ledger must belong to a group
  2. No duplicate ledger names
  3. Inventory Values are Affected = Yes for Purchase & Sales ledgers
  4. Use Bill-by-Bill for Debtors & Creditors only
  5. Opening Balances are optional
  6. Capital is always a liability

5. How to Create a Ledger in Tally Prime (Step-by-Step)

Method 1: Gateway Method

  1. Open Tally Prime
  2. Go to Chart of Accounts → Ledgers → Create
  3. Enter Name, Group, and Properties
  4. Press Ctrl + A to save

Method 2: During Voucher Entry

Type new ledger name → Tally asks “Create?” → Yes → Fill details

Method 3: Multiple Ledger Creation

Chart of Accounts → Ledgers → Create Multiple → Select group → Enter list → Save

9. Common Mistakes and Best Practices

  • Wrong Group → Incorrect financial statements
  • Inventory Affects = No for Sales/Purchase → Stock not updated
  • Bill-by-Bill OFF for Debtors/Creditors → No outstanding reports
  • Duplicate names → Confusion
  • Incorrect opening balance → Trial balance mismatch

10. Revision Notes

  • Ledger = individual account
  • Every ledger must belong to correct group
  • Inventory Affected = Yes → Sales & Purchase
  • Bill-by-Bill = Yes → Debtors/Creditors
  • Capital is always a liability
  • Delete only unused ledgers
