1. Introduction

Contra & Journal are pure accounting vouchers — they never touch inventory.

  • Contra (F4) → Internal movement between Cash & Bank accounts
  • Journal (F7) → All non-cash adjustments & rectifications

2. Contra Voucher (F4)

Used only for internal transfers between Cash & Bank ledgers.

Common Examples:

  • Cash deposited into Bank
  • Cash withdrawn from Bank
  • Bank to Bank transfer
  • Cash transfer between locations

Accounting Rule: One Cash/Bank Dr → Another Cash/Bank Cr

Shortcut: F4

Practical Steps – Contra Voucher

  1. Vouchers → F4
  2. Select Debit Ledger (e.g., HDFC Bank A/c)
  3. Select Credit Ledger (e.g., Cash A/c)
  4. Enter Amount
  5. Narration → “Cash deposited into bank”
  6. Save

3. Journal Voucher (F7)

Used for all non-cash adjustments and rectification entries.

Common Examples:

  • Outstanding expenses / incomes
  • Depreciation
  • Party-to-party adjustments
  • Rectification of wrong entries
  • Accrued interest, provisions

Never involves: Cash | Bank | Stock Items

Shortcut: F7

Practical Steps – Journal Voucher

  1. Vouchers → F7
  2. Select Debit Ledger
  3. Select Credit Ledger
  4. Enter Amount
  5. Narration → “Outstanding rent recorded”
  6. Save

4. Use Cases Linked with Previous Topics

  • Correct wrong Purchase/Sales posting
  • Adjust party balances without cash movement
  • Record depreciation on fixed assets
  • Pass outstanding expense/income entries
  • Rectify ledger mistakes
